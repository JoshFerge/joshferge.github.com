Board of Directors

David Mapel (http://sobek.colorado.edu/~mapel/) Associate Professor of Political Science at the University of Colorado Bouler. Ph.D. Johns Hopkins University 1983. Specializations: contemporary Anglo-American political philosophy; theories of social justice; political obligation and authority; international ethics.  His book, “Social Justice Reconsidered” was published in 1989 among other books and journal publications.

Vanessa Baird (http://polsci.colorado.edu/faculty-staff/faculty/vanessa-baird) Associate Professor of Political Science at the University of Colorado Boulder. Ph.D. University of Houston 2000 Her book, “Answering the Call of the Court: How Justices and Litigants Set the Supreme Court's Agenda”, was published in 2007 by the University of Virginia Press. Website (http://socsci.colorado.edu/~bairdv/)

Horst Mewes (http://polsci.colorado.edu/faculty-staff/faculty/horst-mewes) Associate Professor of Political Science at the University of Colorado Boulder. Ph.D. University of Chicago 1970 Together with Paulo Zagalo e Melo, he is founder and director of futurecitizenship.com (http://www.futurecitizenship.com), an international project consisting of the top scholars in the field of citizenship studies worldwide. The project includes the e-journal Futurecitizens, which he co-edits with a German colleague. Professor Mewes has published both in German and English, including a book on American political thought and politics (in German), articles and book chapters on the German environmental movements, and articles on constitutionalism and political culture in American democracy.

Michelle Ferguson (http://polsci.colorado.edu/faculty-staff/faculty/michaele-ferguson) Assistant Professor of Political Science at the University of Colorado Boulder. Ph.D. Harvard University, 2003 Professor Ferguson is currently working on a book manuscript entitled Sharing Democracy. This book critically examines a dominant presumption in democratic theory that democracies can only work where citizens share something in common – whether that is a nationality, a set of political values, or the status of citizenship. In place of this presumption, Ferguson develops a nonfoundationalist account of how we share democracy with others. This project has been supported by a Visiting Research Fellowship at the University of Utah’s Tanner Humanities Center and a Junior Faculty Development Award from CU. Her work has appeared in Hypatia, Theory & Event, The European Legacy, and Philosophy in Review. Together with Professors Vanessa Baird and David Mapel, she is the recipient of a Ford Foundations Difficult Dialogues Initiative stipend http://www.difficultdialogues.org/projects/and a grant from CU’s Institute for Civic and Ethical Engagement to fund the development of a new service-learning course focused on the cultivation of the dialogue skills necessary for effective citizenship in diverse societies.

Anne Costain (http://polsci.colorado.edu/faculty-staff/faculty/anne-n-costain) Professor of Political Science and an affiliated faculty member in the Women and Gender Studies Program. Ph.D. John Hopkins University She is author of numerous journal articles and chapters in edited books on subjects ranging from women’s activism to social movement politics. Her current research examines non-violence as a social movement tactic and the legacies of the suffrage and anti-slavery movements for later movements challenging sexism and racism in America.

Keller Center Associates

Steve Vanderheiden (http://spot.colorado.edu/~vanders/) Associate Professor of Political Science and Environmental Studies at the University of Colorado Boulder Ph.D. University of Wisconsin-Madison In addition to numerous published articles and book chapters on topics ranging from Rousseau's environmental thought to the politics of SUVs, and edited books on political theory approaches to climate change, energy politics, and environmental rights, his book “Atmospheric Justice: A Political Theory of Climate Change” (Oxford, 2008) won the 2009 Harold and Margaret Sprout award from the International Studies Association for the best book on international environmental politics.

Jennifer Wolak (http://spot.colorado.edu/~wolakj/index.html) Associate Professor of Political Science at the University of Colorado Boulder Ph.D. University of North Carolina 2004 Jennifer’s research interests include political psychology and public opinion. She has published work in the British Journal of Political Science, Political Research Quarterly, and Political Behavior, among others. 

Adam Hosein (http://www.colorado.edu/philosophy/fac_hosein.shtml) Assistant Professor of Philosophy at the University of Colorado Boulder Ph.D. Massachusetts Institute of Technology

Jeff Harden (http://spot.colorado.edu/~jeha9919/) Assistant Professor of Political Science at the University of Colorado Boulder Ph.D. University of North Carolina 2012 Jeff specializes in political methodology and American politics. His research agenda in American politics focuses on political representation, mass/elite linkages, and state politics.

Anand Sokhey (https://sites.google.com/a/colorado.edu/aes/) Assistant Professor of Political Science at the University of Colorado Boulder Ph.D. The Ohio State University 2009 Anand specializes in American politics, and his work examines the role that social influence plays in voting behavior, political participation, and opinion formation, with a special focus on how people employ their networks when acquiring and processing information.
